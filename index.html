About

Noam Chomksy was born December 7, 1928 in Philadelphia. He is sometimes referred to as “the father of modern linguistics”. Chomsky spent much of his life working on linguistics, political activism, and social criticism. He has also contributed greatly to the fields of analytic philosophy and cognitive science.

  • 1945- Chomsky enrolled at the University of Pennsylvania at the age of 16.
  • 1953- Chomsky published his first academic article.
  • 1954- Presented his highly critical ideas about established behaviorist currents in linguistics and was awarded a doctorate of philosophy degree from.
  • 1955- became an assistant professor at MIT.
  • 1961- became a tenured professor in the Department of Modern Languages and Linguistics at MIT.
  • 1962- Chomsky began speaking out against US involvement in the Vietnam war.
